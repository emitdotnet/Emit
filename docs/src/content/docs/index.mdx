## The problem worth solving

Your service just processed a pizza order. You saved it to the database and, in the same breath, tried to publish an event to a message broker. The publish failed. Your database says the order exists. The kitchen heard nothing. Somewhere, a hungry customer is waiting.

This is the **dual-write problem**, and it corrupts systems in ways that are particularly fun to debug at 2am.

## How Emit solves it

Emit implements the **transactional outbox pattern**. Messages are written to a database table inside the same transaction as your business data. A background worker delivers them to the broker, retrying until confirmed. Your handler commits everything or nothing.

The outbox is one of several tools Emit provides. The library also covers in-process request dispatch, distributed coordination, and deep Kafka integration, all sharing the same layered middleware pipeline.

## What's in the box

**Transactional outbox**: atomic message enqueue with your business data, durable background delivery, and ordering guarantees per group key. No message goes missing because a broker hiccupped at the wrong moment.

**In-process mediator**: a typed request/response dispatcher with the same middleware pipeline model as the outbox. Useful for CQRS handlers that also need to publish events.

**Kafka integration**: typed producers, consumer groups with configurable worker pools, fan-out, content-based routing, dead-letter queues, circuit breakers, and rate limiting.

**Pluggable persistence**: MongoDB and PostgreSQL (EF Core) backends. You bring your own client; Emit handles the rest.

**Distributed locks**: built-in leader election and distributed locking, used internally by the outbox worker and available to your own code.

**OpenTelemetry**: distributed tracing with W3C propagation, metrics across every subsystem, and structured logging throughout.
